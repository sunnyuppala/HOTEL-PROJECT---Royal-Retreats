About THE ROYAL RETREATS

Our Story

The Royal Retreats Hotel has quite a fascinating backstory. Did you know that it was originally commissioned by financier Bernhard Beinecke and designed by architects Henry J. Hardenbergh and Thomas Hastings? When it first opened its doors, it quickly became a symbol of opulence and grandeur, attracting high society and celebrities alike. Over the years, the Hotel has witnessed many significant events. In 1920, it became the first hotel in New York City to have a private in-room telephone in every guest room. It also played a role during World War II when it served as a temporary home for troops returning from Europe. Royal Retreats have many branches accross the world and these are built with their own unique specialties.
